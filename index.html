MACHINE TIME
About

Machine Time is a work composed of 119 blocks.

It argues that civilization is reorganizing from human-tempo systems into machine-tempo systems. The world is no longer interpreted first, then acted upon — computation acts first, and meaning follows.

Two substrates anchor the thesis: Bitcoin as an energy/time substrate, and Bittensor as an intelligence/inference substrate.

Read it linearly, or use the index to move by part and chapter. One block per page.

— Stevan / Nemein Press
